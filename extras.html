🌍 Shea's International Recipes

🇹🇷 Turkey – Simit (Turkish Sesame Bagels)

A crunchy, chewy snack from Istanbul's street carts!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1½ tsp dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ cup molasses (or honey)
  • ½ cup sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a big bowl, mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it bubble for 5–10 minutes (that means the yeast is ready!).
  2. Add flour and salt. Mix and knead the dough until smooth—about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Cover the bowl with a towel and let it rest for 1 hour (nap time for the dough).
  4. Divide dough into 4 pieces. Roll each into a rope, then twist the ends together and pinch into a ring.
  5. Mix molasses with ¼ cup water. Dip each ring into the molasses mix, then roll in sesame seeds.
  6. Place on a baking tray. Let rest 15 minutes.
  7. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 15–20 minutes, until golden and crisp.
  8. Let cool just a little... then munch!

🇳🇴 Norway – Kjøttboller (Norwegian Meatballs)

Cozy, spiced meatballs perfect for chilly nights.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or beef/pork mix
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • ½ onion, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp allspice
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper
  • Butter for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix breadcrumbs and milk. Let them sit for 5 minutes (they'll turn mushy—that's good!).
  2. In a big bowl, mix the meat, breadcrumb-milk mix, egg, onion, and spices.
  3. Shape into meatballs—about the size of a ping-pong ball.
  4. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat.
  5. Fry the meatballs in batches, turning often until browned and cooked through (about 10–12 minutes).
  6. Serve with mashed potatoes or gravy... or both!

🇪🇹 Ethiopia – Injera (Teff Flatbread)

Soft and spongy bread that doubles as a utensil!

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups teff flour
  • 2 cups water
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix teff flour and water in a bowl. Stir until smooth.
  2. Cover with a cloth and let it sit at room temp for 1–3 days (it will smell a little tangy—that's okay!).
  3. When you're ready to cook, stir in a pinch of salt.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. No oil needed!
  5. Pour about ½ cup batter into the pan and swirl to coat the bottom like a pancake.
  6. Cook for 1–2 minutes—bubbles will form on top. Do not flip!
  7. When the edges lift and it looks dry on top, slide it out.
  8. Cool on a cloth (not a plate—it'll get soggy!).
  9. Eat with stew, curry, or just plain—it's fun to tear and scoop!
